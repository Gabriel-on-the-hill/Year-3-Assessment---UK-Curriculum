## ✏️ Q17 — Inference *(How did Maya feel? Use evidence from the passage.)*

**Maximum: 3 marks**

Use the rubric below to award marks. **Each mark is independent** — a student can earn 1, 2 or 3 depending on which criteria they meet.

### Mark 1 — Identifies a Feeling (1 mark)
Pupil names a plausible feeling that fits the context. Acceptable answers include:
- **Sad / upset / unhappy** ✅
- **Worried / disappointed / devastated / heartbroken** ✅
- **Distressed / shocked / hurt** ✅
- *Just "bad" or "not happy"* — award the mark with caveat (note: emerging vocabulary)

❌ Not awarded for: angry (limited textual support), confused (no support), happy (clearly wrong)

### Mark 2 — Cites Textual Evidence (1 mark)
Pupil references at least one of the following from the passage:
- **"her eyes filling with tears"** — strongest evidence
- **"Oh no!" / her cry**
- **"three whole weeks saving her pocket money"** — implies the kite mattered to her
- **"watched helplessly"** / **"snapped"**
- A general reference to "she had been saving up" or "she was sad about her kite"

❌ Not awarded for: vague references to "the story" without a specific detail.

### Mark 3 — Connects Evidence to Feeling (1 mark)
Pupil explicitly links the evidence to the feeling. Look for words such as:
- "I think she was sad **because** her eyes were filling with tears."
- "She felt upset, **as shown by** 'Oh no!'"
- Implicit but clear connection: "She cried 'Oh no!' so she must have felt really sad."

❌ Not awarded for: a feeling and an unconnected piece of evidence presented as separate facts.

### Sample Responses

| Response | Marks | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| *"Sad."* | 1/3 | Feeling only, no evidence, no connection |
| *"She was sad. Her eyes were filling with tears."* | 2/3 | Feeling + evidence, but connection is implicit at best |
| *"Maya felt very upset because the passage says her eyes were filling with tears and she had spent three weeks saving."* | 3/3 | Full connection with two pieces of evidence |
| *"She felt happy because the kite was flying."* | 0/3 | Wrong inference — does not match the moment described |

## 🔮 Q19 — Prediction *(What happens next? Give one reason.)*

**Maximum: 3 marks**

### Mark 1 — Plausible Prediction (1 mark)
Pupil predicts an outcome that fits the story. Most likely:
- **Mr Okafor returns the kite** ✅
- Mr Okafor gives Maya something nice ✅
- Maya is happy / surprised / relieved ✅
- Maya thanks Mr Okafor ✅

❌ Not awarded for: predictions that contradict the text (e.g. "the kite is destroyed" — the passage explicitly says he is holding "something red and smiling broadly").

### Mark 2 — References Specific Detail (1 mark)
Pupil references one of these textual clues:
- **"holding something red"** — strongest signal it is the kite
- **"smiling broadly"** — suggests good news
- **"I believe this belongs to you"** — direct quote pointing to return
- **The setting (gate, neighbour)** — geographic plausibility

❌ Not awarded for: vague reasoning ("because of the story" or "because that's what happens").

### Mark 3 — Logical, Clear Reasoning (1 mark)
Pupil's reasoning is coherent and well-expressed. The connection between detail and prediction is explicit.

### Sample Responses

| Response | Marks | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| *"He gives her the kite."* | 1/3 | Plausible prediction, no evidence cited |
| *"He gives the kite back because he is holding something red."* | 3/3 | Plausible + evidence + clear connection |
| *"Maya will be happy because Mr Okafor is smiling and holding red."* | 3/3 | Slightly indirect but full credit — all 3 criteria met |
| *"Mr Okafor steals it."* | 0/3 | Contradicts the textual evidence (smiling, "I believe this belongs to you") |

## 📝 Q26 — Writing Task *(Continue the story in 3–4 sentences)*

**Maximum: 10 marks** across 5 categories (2 marks each)

> Mark holistically — read the whole response first, then award marks across each category. Be generous where there is genuine effort, and look for evidence of taught Year 3 skills.

### Category 1 — Content & Coherence (2 marks)

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 2 | Engages strongly with the prompt; ideas develop logically; the continuation feels like a natural extension of the story; appropriate to genre (mystery / adventure suggested by the prompt) |
| 1 | Engages with the prompt; ideas are present but limited or somewhat disjointed; mostly coherent |
| 0 | Off-topic, lifts/copies the prompt only, or written content is too brief to assess (one sentence or fewer) |

### Category 2 — Sentence Structure (2 marks)

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 2 | Variety of sentence types (mix of simple and complex); at least one sentence uses a Year 3 conjunction (*because, when, although, if, before, after, while, so*); sentences are complete |
| 1 | Mostly simple sentences; one attempt at variety OR one conjunction used; mostly complete sentences |
| 0 | Sentences are repetitive, fragmented, or run-on throughout |

### Category 3 — Punctuation (2 marks)

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 2 | Capital letters and full stops mostly accurate; **at least one** higher punctuation feature attempted (inverted commas, comma in a list, exclamation/question mark, apostrophe) |
| 1 | Capital letters and full stops mostly accurate; no higher punctuation attempted OR attempted but with several errors |
| 0 | Capital letters and full stops missing or incorrect throughout |

### Category 4 — Vocabulary (2 marks)

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 2 | At least 2–3 ambitious or interesting word choices (e.g. powerful verbs like *crept, dashed, gasped*; vivid adjectives like *enormous, gleaming, ancient*); avoidance of repetitive bland words |
| 1 | Some attempt at varied vocabulary; one or two ambitious words; mostly common words |
| 0 | Very limited vocabulary; words like *got, said, nice, thing* used repeatedly with no variation |

### Category 5 — Spelling (2 marks)

| Marks | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| 2 | Common Year 3 words (including from the statutory list) mostly correct; phonetically reasonable attempts at unfamiliar words |
| 1 | Some Year 3/4 statutory words misspelt; phonetic attempts on harder words; meaning still clear |
| 0 | Spelling errors are frequent and interfere with meaning |

### Bonus Diagnostic Indicators *(not scored but record on the report)*

Note whether the student attempts:
- ☐ A new paragraph
- ☐ Inverted commas / direct speech
- ☐ Description of a setting
- ☐ Description of a character (named or implied)
- ☐ A clear narrative arc beyond just the next sentence

These indicators are strong predictors of Summer term writing readiness.

## 🎯 Tips for Marking the Open-Ended Questions

1. **Read all three open-ended responses first** before assigning any marks. This helps calibrate against the student's overall ability.
2. **Mark generously where intent is clear.** A child who writes "she was sad cuz of her tears" still meets the inference criteria — don't penalise for spelling/punctuation in inference and prediction (those are scored separately in Q26).
3. **Be strict for Q26 spelling and punctuation** — those are taught Year 3 skills and worth a fair check.
4. **Document specific quotes** in the report so the student's responses are traceable. The CSV/JSON export from the assessment has the full text.
5. **If you are unsure, mark and move on**, then return after marking all three. A second pass usually clarifies borderline cases.
